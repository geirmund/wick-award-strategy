01 — Introduction

Our journey and evolving purpose

Wick Award was established in 2015 as a resident-led Big Local project, empowered with £1 million in National Lottery funding to make Hackney Wick a better place to live. In July 2025, we incorporated as a CIO — securing our legacy and expanding our reach.

Our Legacy (2015–2024)

For ten years, we served as a trusted connector, investor, and advocate — nurturing grassroots initiatives and ensuring residents have a powerful voice in shaping their neighbourhood. Three community-identified priorities guided our work: young people, health & wellbeing, and community connections.

Strategic Expansion

Incorporation as a CIO broadens our geographical focus to the Well Street Common Neighbourhood — now covering the wards of Hackney Wick, Victoria, and Morningside. We are applying our proven, resident-led model to a wider community.

The Community Context

52.1% of households are social renters — more than double the London average. The area has higher deprivation, lower employment, and higher rates of disability than the borough average. These are the lived realities driving our mandate.

02 — Strategic Foundation

Vision, Mission & Principles

This is our organisation's North Star — the fundamental purpose, values, and principles that guide every decision, partnership, and initiative we undertake.

Our Vision

For the Well Street Common Neighbourhood to be a place where residents and organisations feel empowered to work together to build resilience, raise aspirations, and break down barriers — creating a genuine sense of community for the long term.

Our Mission

To provide an open and accessible forum for residents, linking the Well Street Common Neighbourhood's rich and diverse communities to collectively define their priorities and support initiatives designed to meet community needs — acting as an interface between the community and social providers.

Resident-led

Our direction is set by the priorities and lived experiences of local people. Those most affected by issues are best placed to design the solutions.

Person-centred

We listen to, respond to, and involve residents in all decisions — rooted in empathy, respect, and a deep understanding of individual needs.

Community Initiative

We empower and support home-grown, hyperlocal projects. We take risks on good ideas from passionate local people who might otherwise not receive funding.

Long-term

We are committed to building sustainable change and trusted relationships over time — patient, consistent investment in community capacity.

Inclusive

We actively strive to ensure our work reflects the voices of everyone in our diverse community, breaking down barriers and championing equity.

Flexible & Responsive

We remain agile, adapting our strategies and activities to meet emerging community needs and respond to new evidence and opportunities.

03 — Strategic Landscape

Challenges & Opportunities

A clear-eyed understanding of our strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats — the foundation for realistic goals and effective strategies.

Strengths

  • Ten-year legacy as a unique, trusted connector
  • Resident-led with a "dispersed model of leadership"
  • Evidence-based approach grounded in community research
  • Strong networks across VCS and statutory bodies

Weaknesses

  • Limited track record in diversified fundraising
  • Governance diversity gap — trustee board not fully representative
  • Operational dependence on key personnel

Opportunities

  • NHS Integrated Care Systems prioritising place-based, preventative work
  • CIO status enables expanded mandate and partnerships
  • Growing interest from public sector in community-led models

Threats

  • Highly competitive funding environment in the Hackney VCS
  • Risk of losing key staff and institutional knowledge
  • Transition from Big Local single-stream funding model

05 — Organisational Development

Building our capacity for impact

Achieving our external mission depends critically on our internal strength. Three targeted priority areas will ensure we are fit for purpose and accountable.

Identified Gap

Enhancing Diversity & Inclusion

Why It Matters

Lack of diverse representation in governance risks undermining our connection to all parts of the community and our legitimacy as a resident-led organisation.

Our Solution

Actively recruit trustees, advisors, and members with local lived experience who represent the diverse communities of the Well Street Common Neighbourhood.

Identified Gap

Improving Strategic Storytelling

Why It Matters

We need to continuously and compellingly communicate our unique role as a "humble enabler" and catalyst — to funders, partners, and residents alike.

Our Solution

Secure funding for a communications and social media content creator role to develop engaging digital content and manage our channels effectively.

Identified Gap

Strengthening Fundraising Capability

Why It Matters

We need dedicated fundraising support and a clear strategy for replacing the Big Local "Community Chest" model to build a sustainable, diverse funding base.

Our Solution

Mobilise trustees to actively support funding bids and invest in bid-writing training — including exploring the potential of AI support tools.

07 — Governance & Evaluation

Strong oversight, continuous learning

Our governance, risk management, and evaluation framework is the bedrock of our organisation — essential for maintaining trust with community, partners, and funders.

Board of Trustees

Holds legal responsibility for strategic direction, financial health, and regulatory compliance. Conducts quarterly reviews of this strategic framework to analyse performance and capture learning.

Partners & Community

A broad network of community representatives providing invaluable insight, articulating community needs, and ensuring our work remains relevant and grounded in lived experience.

Project Worker

The operational link between people and organisations — driving the day-to-day work that builds connections and makes long-term impact in the neighbourhood.

Risk Management

Potential Risk

Trustee body lacks representation and expertise

Mitigation

Create a representative structure with a competence framework; promote roles to under-represented groups; implement comprehensive training and inclusive recruitment processes.

Potential Risk

Inability to raise sufficient grant funds

Mitigation

Engage professional fundraising support where needed; implement robust budgeting; ensure strong stewardship and impact reporting to funders.

Potential Risk

Loss of key staff and corporate knowledge

Mitigation

Develop a clear succession plan; meticulously document all systems and project knowledge; implement cross-training programmes to build shared capacity.

Potential Risk

Charity lacks clear strategic direction

Mitigation

Involve all key stakeholders in the creation and monitoring of financial and operational plans; collect regular feedback from community members and funders.

Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning

We will implement a new MEL framework with clear KPIs developed at our October 2025 strategic workshop, tracked quarterly by the trustee board. Community engagement survey scores will measure our effectiveness in empowering residents. This plan is designed to be a dynamic, living document that adapts to the evolving needs of our community.
